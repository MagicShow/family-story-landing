The Story

The Johnson Family Journey

"They left Illinois in April of 1852. Oxen. Wagons. A newborn. Six months and a thousand miles later, they reached the Salt Lake Valley — and built something that outlasted them all."

The Johnson family didn't set out to become pioneers. They set out to find a home. Like thousands of others in the mid-1800s, they packed everything they owned into a single wagon and headed west — toward a life they could only imagine.

The journey took six months. One of their children was born on the trail. Two family members didn't survive. But they kept going — because that's what families do. They endure. They adapt. They carry each other forward.

Today, the Johnson's descendants number in the hundreds. They live across the country. Most never knew the full story of how their family got here — until someone finally asked the right questions and recorded the answers.

⚜

Every family has a journey like this. Family Story helps you find it — and preserve it.
